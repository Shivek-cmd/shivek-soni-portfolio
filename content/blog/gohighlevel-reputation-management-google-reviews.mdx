## Building an Automated Review Generation System

### Step 1: Set Up the Review Request Trigger

The best time to ask for a review is immediately after a positive experience — when the customer is happiest. In GoHighLevel, create a workflow with this trigger:

**Trigger options:**
- Pipeline stage change to "Completed" or "Delivered"
- Tag added: "service-complete"
- Manual trigger by team member after positive interaction
- Invoice paid (for product businesses)

### Step 2: Build the Sentiment Filter

This is the secret to maintaining a high star rating. Before sending someone directly to Google, check their satisfaction first:

1. **Send satisfaction survey via SMS**: "Hi [Name], how was your experience with us? Reply with a number 1-10"
2. **If response is 9-10**: Send Google Review link immediately
3. **If response is 7-8**: Send follow-up asking what could be improved, then send review link
4. **If response is 1-6**: Alert team for immediate outreach — do NOT send review link

This two-step process ensures that only happy customers land on your Google Review page, while unhappy customers get personal attention before they leave a negative public review.

> The biggest mistake businesses make with review automation is sending every customer directly to Google. One angry customer can undo months of positive reviews. Always filter sentiment first.

### Step 3: Craft the Review Request Message

Your review request should be personal, specific, and make it as easy as possible to leave a review:

**SMS template (keep under 160 characters):**
"Hi [First Name], thanks for choosing [Business Name]! Could you share your experience? Takes 30 seconds: [Google Review Link]"

**Email template:**
- Subject: "Quick favor, [First Name]?"
- Personal greeting referencing the specific service they received
- One-sentence explanation of why reviews matter to your business
- Large, prominent button linking directly to Google Review page
- Thank you note

**Key principle:** Remove every possible barrier. The review link should open directly to the Google review form — not your Google Business Profile page where they have to find the review button themselves.

### Step 4: Follow Up (Once)

If someone doesn't leave a review after the first request, send one follow-up 3-5 days later. Never send more than two requests — beyond that, you're annoying people.

**Follow-up SMS**: "Hi [First Name], just a gentle reminder — your review helps other [industry] find great service. Here's the link: [Google Review Link]. No pressure!"

## Responding to Reviews: Best Practices

Getting reviews is half the battle. Responding to them is equally important for SEO and trust:

**For positive reviews (4-5 stars):**
- Thank the reviewer by name
- Reference something specific about their experience
- Mention your business name and key service (helps with keyword relevance)
- Keep it genuine — avoid copy-paste responses

**Example:** "Thank you, Sarah! We're glad the CRM setup is already helping your team track leads more effectively. It was great working with you on the automation workflows. — Shivek, Automate with Level"

**For negative reviews (1-3 stars):**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Acknowledge their frustration without being defensive
- Take the conversation offline ("Please call us at...")
- Show other readers that you take feedback seriously

GoHighLevel's review response templates help you respond quickly while maintaining a personal touch. Set up 4-5 templates for common scenarios and customize them for each review.

## Measuring Reputation Performance

Track these metrics monthly:

- **Total reviews**: Aim for 5-10 new reviews per month minimum
- **Average rating**: Maintain 4.5+ stars
- **Response rate**: Respond to 100% of reviews within 48 hours
- **Review request conversion rate**: How many requests result in actual reviews (target: 20-30%)
- **Local search ranking**: Track your Google Maps position for key terms
