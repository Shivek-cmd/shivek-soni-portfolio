## Step 1: Sub-Account Setup and Business Profile

Before building anything, get your foundation right.

In GHL, every local business lives inside a **sub-account**. This is where all your contacts, funnels, automations, and communications are stored.

What to configure first:
- Business name, address, and phone number (exact match to Google Business Profile)
- Upload your logo and set brand colors
- Connect your custom domain
- Set your timezone and business hours
- Add your business email and configure LC Email for deliverability

**Pro tip:** Your business name, address, and phone number inside GHL must exactly match what's on your Google Business Profile. Consistency across platforms is a local SEO signal Google actually checks.

## Step 2: Connect Your Phone System

This is where most local businesses see their fastest win.

GHL's **Missed Call Text-Back** is one of its most powerful features for local businesses. When a customer calls and nobody answers, GHL automatically sends them a text within seconds keeping the conversation alive instead of losing the lead to a competitor.

Setup steps:
- Purchase a local phone number inside GHL (same area code as your business)
- Enable the Missed Call Text-Back workflow (built-in template)
- Set your auto-reply message — keep it personal and specific
- Connect call tracking to your CRM so every call logs automatically

For businesses getting 20+ calls per week, this single feature often recovers 15-20% of previously lost leads immediately.

## Step 3: CRM and Pipeline Configuration

A local business pipeline is simple but needs to be right.

Build a pipeline that mirrors how your business actually works. For most local service businesses, a 5-stage pipeline works well:

1. **New Lead** — just came in, not yet contacted
2. **Contacted** — reached out, waiting for response
3. **Appointment Set** — booked, not yet completed
4. **Job Completed** — service delivered
5. **Review Requested** — post-job follow-up sent

Add custom fields that matter for your business — service type, property address, job value, lead source. This data powers your automations later and gives you real visibility into your pipeline.

## Step 4: Booking and Calendar System

Local businesses run on appointments. GHL's calendar system handles the entire booking flow — from availability to confirmation to reminders — automatically.

Configure your calendar:
- Set your available days and hours
- Add buffer time between appointments
- Enable online booking (embed on your website or funnel)
- Turn on confirmation emails and SMS on booking
- Set up 24-hour and 2-hour reminder sequences
- Build a no-show recovery workflow that triggers if the customer doesn't confirm

The reminder sequence alone typically reduces no-show rates from 30-35% down to 10-12% for most local businesses. That's real revenue recovered with zero manual effort.

## Step 5: Lead Capture and Speed-to-Lead Automation

The biggest conversion killer for local businesses is slow response time. Research consistently shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes makes you dramatically more likely to close the deal compared to waiting even 30 minutes.

Build a speed-to-lead automation:
- **Trigger:** New lead submits a form or calls
- **Step 1:** Instant SMS to the lead (within 60 seconds)
- **Step 2:** Automated email with your business details (2 minutes)
- **Step 3:** Internal notification to your team for a personal call
- **Step 4:** If no response in 2 hours, AI follow-up SMS
- **Step 5:** Continue multi-touch follow-up over 7-14 days

This workflow runs 24/7 — even when you're on a job, asleep, or on weekends when most of your competitors are unreachable.

## Step 6: Reputation Management and Google Reviews

For local businesses, Google reviews are currency. More reviews and a higher star rating means higher Google Maps rankings, which means more free leads every month.

Build an automated review generation system:
- Trigger a satisfaction survey via SMS after every completed job
- Happy customers (score 9-10) receive a direct Google Review link
- Neutral customers (7-8) get a personal follow-up before the review ask
- Unhappy customers (1-6) trigger an immediate manager alert for recovery

This system runs without you thinking about it. One client went from 23 reviews at 3.8 stars to 89 reviews at 4.7 stars in just 90 days — moving from position 7 to position 2 in Google Maps for their main keywords.

## Step 7: Local SEO Setup Inside GHL

GHL has a built-in Local SEO dashboard powered by Search Atlas. For local businesses this is incredibly valuable.

What to configure:
- Connect your Google Business Profile to GHL
- Add your target keywords (format: "service + city" e.g. "plumber in Austin")
- Set up heatmap tracking to see where you rank across your service area
- Enable automated weekly local SEO reports
- Use the GMB integration to post updates, offers, and photos directly from GHL

Green pins on your heatmap mean you rank in the top 3 for that location. Red pins show where you need work. This gives you a clear, visual picture of your local search visibility that you can improve week over week.

## Step 8: Social Media and Content Automation

GHL's Social Planner lets you schedule and publish content across all platforms from one place — Facebook, Instagram, Google Business Profile, LinkedIn, and more.
