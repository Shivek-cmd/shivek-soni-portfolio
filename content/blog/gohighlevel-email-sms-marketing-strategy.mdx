## The Foundation: Segmentation Before Sending

Before you send a single email or SMS, you need to segment your contact list. Sending the same message to everyone is the fastest way to tank your deliverability and annoy your audience.

**Essential segments to create in GoHighLevel:**

- **By lead source**: Contacts from Facebook Ads get different messaging than referrals or organic leads
- **By pipeline stage**: A new lead needs education; a qualified prospect needs social proof; a lost deal needs re-engagement
- **By engagement level**: Active openers vs. inactive contacts (haven't opened in 30+ days)
- **By purchase history**: First-time buyers vs. repeat customers vs. never purchased
- **By interest/service**: Segment by which service or product they've shown interest in

GoHighLevel makes segmentation easy with smart lists — dynamic contact lists that update automatically based on tags, custom fields, and behavior. Set these up once, and your segments stay current forever.

## Building Your Email Marketing Engine

### Campaign Type 1: Welcome Sequence (Days 1-7)

Every new contact should enter a welcome sequence immediately. This is your chance to make a strong first impression and set expectations:

- **Email 1 (Immediate)**: Welcome + deliver promised lead magnet or resource. Keep it short and warm.
- **Email 2 (Day 2)**: Your story — who you are, why you do what you do, and who you help. Build connection.
- **Email 3 (Day 4)**: Best piece of content — your most helpful blog post, video, or guide. Deliver pure value.
- **Email 4 (Day 6)**: Social proof — customer story or case study showing real results.
- **Email 5 (Day 7)**: Soft CTA — invite them to book a call, attend a webinar, or explore your services.

### Campaign Type 2: Nurture Sequence (Ongoing)

After the welcome sequence, contacts enter a long-term nurture campaign. This is where most businesses drop the ball — they stop communicating after the initial sequence.

Send one email per week minimum:
- **Week 1**: Educational content (how-to guide related to their interest)
- **Week 2**: Industry insight or trend analysis
- **Week 3**: Behind-the-scenes or personal story
- **Week 4**: Case study or client success story
- **Repeat cycle** with fresh content

### Campaign Type 3: Re-engagement Sequence

Contacts who haven't opened an email in 30+ days need a different approach:

- **Email 1**: "We miss you" with a compelling reason to re-engage (new resource, updated service, special offer)
- **Email 2 (3 days later)**: Direct question — "Is this still relevant to you?"
- **Email 3 (7 days later)**: Final notice — "We'll remove you from our list unless we hear back"
- **No response**: Remove from active email list (keeps your deliverability high)

## SMS Marketing: The Revenue Multiplier

SMS has a 98% open rate compared to email's 20-25%. That's not a typo. When you need someone to actually see your message, SMS wins every time.

**But SMS requires a different approach than email:**

- Keep messages under 160 characters when possible
- Always identify yourself (e.g., "Hi [Name], it's Shivek from Automate with Level")
- Include a clear, single CTA
- Respect timing — send during business hours only (9am-7pm recipient's time zone)
- Always provide an opt-out option

**High-performing SMS use cases in GoHighLevel:**

1. **Appointment reminders**: Reduce no-shows by 60-80%
2. **Flash offers**: Time-sensitive promotions (24-48 hour windows)
3. **Follow-up nudges**: "Just checking in — did you get a chance to review the proposal?"
4. **Review requests**: Direct link to Google Review page after service completion
5. **Event invitations**: Webinar or workshop registrations

## Automation: Putting It All Together

The real power of GoHighLevel marketing is in the automation layer. Here's how to connect everything:

**Trigger-based campaigns:**
- Contact fills out form → Welcome sequence starts
- Contact books appointment → Appointment reminder SMS sequence starts
- Contact reaches "Proposal Sent" pipeline stage → Case study email sends
- Contact hasn't engaged in 14 days → Re-engagement sequence triggers
- Contact leaves a 5-star review → Thank you SMS + referral request email

**Behavior-based branching:**
- If contact opens email but doesn't click → Send SMS with same CTA
- If contact clicks link but doesn't purchase → Send reminder with urgency
- If contact replies to SMS → Route to human team member immediately

## Measuring What Matters

Track these metrics weekly:

- **Email open rate**: Target 25%+ (industry average is 21%)
- **Email click rate**: Target 3%+ (industry average is 2.6%)
- **SMS response rate**: Target 15%+
- **Unsubscribe rate**: Keep under 0.5% per campaign
- **Revenue attributed to email/SMS**: Track with UTM parameters and GHL attribution

The businesses that win at marketing are the ones that track, test, and iterate consistently. Set up a weekly review cadence and adjust your campaigns based on real data.
