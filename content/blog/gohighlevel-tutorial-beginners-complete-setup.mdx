## Getting Started with GoHighLevel: What You Need to Know

GoHighLevel is one of the most powerful all-in-one marketing and CRM platforms available today — but that power comes with complexity. If you're logging into GHL for the first time, the sheer number of features, menus, and settings can feel overwhelming.

This tutorial cuts through the noise. I'll walk you through the exact setup process I use with every new client — the same process that gets businesses from zero to fully operational in GoHighLevel within a week, not months.

Whether you're an agency setting up GHL for the first time, or a business owner who just purchased a subscription, this guide covers everything you need to get started the right way.

## Step 1: Understand the Account Structure

Before you click anything, understand how GoHighLevel is organized:

**Agency Account** (the top level):
- This is your main dashboard
- You manage billing, users, and global settings here
- You create sub-accounts from here

**Sub-Accounts** (where the work happens):
- Each client or business gets its own sub-account
- All CRM data, funnels, automations, and settings live inside sub-accounts
- Sub-accounts are completely isolated from each other

**Why this matters:** If you're a single business owner, you'll work primarily inside one sub-account. If you're an agency, you'll create a separate sub-account for each client. Understanding this hierarchy prevents confusion later.

## Step 2: Create and Configure Your Sub-Account

Navigate to Settings → Sub-Accounts → Create New Sub-Account. Fill in:

- **Business name**: Your actual business name (or your client's)
- **Business type**: Select the closest match from the dropdown
- **Address and phone**: Important for local SEO and Google Business Profile integration
- **Timezone**: Critical for scheduling and automation timing
- **Industry**: Helps GHL suggest relevant templates

**Pro tip:** Use a consistent naming convention for sub-accounts if you manage multiple businesses. I recommend: `[Business Name] - [Location/Market]` — for example, "Smith Dental - Austin TX."

## Step 3: Connect Your Domain

Your funnels, websites, and booking pages need a custom domain to look professional. Here's how to set it up:

1. Go to Settings → Domains → Add Custom Domain
2. Enter your domain (e.g., `app.yourbusiness.com` or `go.yourbusiness.com`)
3. GHL will give you a CNAME record to add to your DNS
4. Go to your domain registrar (GoDaddy, Namecheap, Cloudflare) and add the CNAME
5. Wait for DNS propagation (usually 15-60 minutes)
6. Set as default domain for all future funnels and pages

**Important:** Use a subdomain (like `app.` or `go.`) rather than your root domain. This keeps your main website separate from your GHL assets and avoids DNS conflicts.

## Step 4: Set Up Phone Numbers and Messaging

GoHighLevel's communication features require phone numbers:

1. Go to Settings → Phone Numbers → Add Number
2. Choose a local number with your area code (better for local businesses) or a toll-free number
3. Enable both calling and messaging capabilities
4. Set up A2P 10DLC registration (required for business SMS in the USA)

**A2P registration is mandatory.** Without it, your SMS messages will be filtered or blocked by carriers. The registration process takes 1-3 weeks for approval, so start this immediately — don't wait until you need to send your first campaign.

## Step 5: Connect Essential Integrations

GoHighLevel connects with dozens of external tools. Set up these integrations first:

**Must-have integrations:**
- **Google Business Profile**: For review management and Google messaging
- **Facebook/Instagram**: For lead ads, Messenger, and social media management
- **Google Calendar**: For two-way calendar sync with appointments
- **Stripe**: For payment processing on funnels and invoices
- **Mailgun or SMTP**: For custom email sending (better deliverability than default)

**Nice-to-have integrations:**
- **Zoom or Google Meet**: For automated meeting links
- **QuickBooks**: For invoice and payment tracking
- **Zapier or Make**: For connecting non-native tools
- **Google Analytics**: For advanced funnel tracking

Each integration has its own setup process, but GHL provides step-by-step guides within the integration settings. Take the time to set these up correctly — broken integrations cause headaches down the road.

## Step 6: Build Your First Automation

Now that the foundation is set, let's build a simple but powerful automation to prove the concept.

**The "New Lead Welcome" Automation:**

1. Go to Automations → Create Workflow → Start from Scratch
2. **Trigger**: Contact Created (filter: has tag "new-lead")
3. **Action 1**: Wait 1 minute (gives time for contact data to sync)
4. **Action 2**: Send SMS — "Hi [First Name], thanks for your interest! I'll be in touch shortly. - [Your Name]"
5. **Action 3**: Send Email — Welcome email with your branding and next steps
6. **Action 4**: Create Task — "Follow up with [Contact Name]" assigned to you, due in 1 hour
7. **Action 5**: Add to Pipeline → "New Lead Qualification" → Stage: "New Lead"

This single automation ensures that every new lead gets an immediate response, enters your CRM pipeline, and creates a task so nothing falls through the cracks. It takes 10 minutes to build and saves hours of manual work every week.

## Step 7: Set Up Calendars and Booking

GoHighLevel's calendar system lets prospects book appointments directly:

1. Go to Calendars → Create Calendar
2. Choose calendar type (Round Robin, Collective, Class Booking, or Personal)
3. Set your availability (days, hours, timezone)
4. Configure buffer time between appointments (I recommend 15 minutes minimum)
5. Set up confirmation and reminder automations (see Step 6 pattern)
6. Embed the calendar on your funnel or website

**The goal:** Remove all friction from the booking process. The fewer steps between "I'm interested" and "appointment confirmed," the more appointments you'll get.

## Common Setup Mistakes to Avoid

After setting up hundreds of GoHighLevel accounts, these are the mistakes I see most often:

1. **Skipping A2P registration** — Your SMS will fail when you need it most
2. **Using the default email domain** — Hurts deliverability; always set up custom SMTP
3. **Not mapping your process first** — You'll rebuild everything in 3 months
4. **Creating too many pipelines** — Start simple, expand as needed
5. **Ignoring user permissions** — Set up proper roles before giving team access
